Jerk Chicken

Taste the Caribbean heat.

spicy show-off
Effort●●●
Serves4–6
OriginJamaica

Ingredients

  • The marinade
  • The rest

As always: I hate measuring. Take all units with a smidgen/pinch/dash of salt.

Method

Before you start: wear gloves for the scotch bonnets. This is not a suggestion — it's advice from someone who learned the hard way.

  1. Blitz the marinade

    Everything from the first list goes into a blender — scotch bonnets, scallions, garlic, thyme, ginger, allspice, brown sugar, soy, lime, oil. Blend until it's a rough paste. It should smell like a holiday.

  2. Marinate overnight

    Coat the chicken thoroughly, getting some under the skin. Seal it up and refrigerate overnight — 24 hours is even better.

    Patience here is 80% of the recipe.

  3. Set up the grill

    Charcoal, two zones: hot side and cool side. If you can get your hands on pimento wood, you win. Otherwise a chunk of any mild smoking wood does nicely.

  4. Cook low, finish hot

    Start the thighs on the cool side, lid on, until nearly cooked through. Then move them over the coals to char the skin. Turn often; the sugar in the marinade wants to burn.

  5. Rest and serve

    Rest a few minutes, then chop and pile onto a platter. Rice and peas on the side, extra lime over the top.

    Pairs well with a cold drink and standing around the grill longer than necessary.
